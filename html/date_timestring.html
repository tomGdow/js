Introduction

  1. There are many ways to format a date as a string
  2. The JavaScript specification only specifies one format to be universally supported: the date time string format, a simplification of the ISO 8601 calendar date extended format
  3. The format is as follows 
    YYYY-MM-DDTHH:mm:ss.sssZ

Date Time String Format

YYYY
  1. The year
  2. Four digits (0000 to 9999)
  3. Or as an expanded year of + or - followed by six digits
  4. The sign is required for expanded years
  5. -000000 is explicitly disallowed as a valid year
MM
  1. MM is the month
  2. Two digits (01 to 12)
  3. Defaults to 01
DD
  1. The day of the month
  2. Two digits (01 to 31)
  3. Defaults to 01
T
  1. T is a literal character, which indicates the beginning of the time part of the string.
  2. The T is required when specifying the time part
HH
  1. The hour
  2. Two digits (00 to 23)
  3. As a special case, 24:00:00 is allowed, and is interpreted as midnight at the beginning of the next day
  4. Defaults to 00
mm
  1. The minute
  2. Two digits (00 to 59)
  3. Defaults to 00
ss
  1. The second
  2. Two digits (00 to 59)
  3. Defaults to 00
sss
  1. The millisecond
  2. With three digits (000 to 999)
  3. Defaults to 000
Z
  1. The timezone offset
  2. Can either be the literal character Z,indicating UTC
  3. Or can be + or - followed by HH:mm, the offset in hours and minutes from UTC
